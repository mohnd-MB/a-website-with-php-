Medicine

The specialty of "human medicine" is defined as the specialty that is concerned with diagnosing pathological conditions and proposing treatment for them. In addition, it is the specialty that deals with the prevention and treatment of diseases through various medical methods. The human medicine specialty aims to graduate doctors who care about human health and well-being.

Dentist

Dentistry, also known as oral medicine, is the branch of medicine that consists of the study, diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of diseases, disorders, and conditions of the oral cavity, typically of the teeth (development and arrangement of teeth) as well as the oral mucosa and adjacent and associated structures and tissues, particularly in the maxillofacial region.

Labrotory

It is one of the departments of the College of Medical Sciences, and this specialty works briefly in analyzing biological disease samples such as urine samples, blood, tissues, and other samples, using advanced equipment such as microscopes, in order to help doctors diagnose the various diseases that appear through these analyzes and then treat them

Nutration

Therapeutic nutrition is the use of food as a therapeutic method and the use of nutrients in food to overcome many health problems and prevent them. It is essential in the treatment of many diseases, and sometimes it is the only way to treat them
